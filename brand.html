Our Story

Obsession
is the only
standard.

Liquisity began with a single question: why should a case be an afterthought? The answer took two years, twelve alloy formulations, and a workshop that opens before dawn.

The Foundation

Made by fewer
than eight people.

Every Liquisity case passes through fewer than eight pairs of hands. We keep it that way deliberately. Scale is the enemy of craft — and we are not a scale business.

We do not outsource polishing. We do not use coatings. The chrome you see is the alloy itself, coaxed into reflectivity over six hours of progressive polishing across twelve grit stages.

If a case doesn't pass our 14-point inspection, it goes back to the polishing bench. Not to a discount bin. Not to an outlet. Back to the bench.

How it's made

Three stages.
No shortcuts.

01 — Mill

Machine

Each case starts as a solid billet of ZA-8 aerospace-grade alloy. CNC milled to ±0.01mm over four hours — the tolerance required for a seamless acoustic seal.

02 — Finish

Polish

Six further hours of hand-polishing, moving through twelve progressive grit stages. No lacquer. No coating. The reflectivity is in the metal itself — earned, not applied.

03 — QC

Inspect

Every case passes a 14-point quality check under controlled lighting. Surface uniformity, hinge tension, lid retention, acoustic fit. Those that fall short are re-polished, not discarded.
